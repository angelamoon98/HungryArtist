Karen Healy

About Karen

Karen Healy is a documentary photographer and artist based in North Carolina. Through portraiture and environmental landscapes, her work explores quiet moments that illuminate the larger story. She has a Certificate in Documentary Arts from the Center for Documentary Studies at Duke University and a B.A. in Communication Studies from the University of Massachusetts. Karen grew up in New England and moved to North Carolina in 1993 with her husband and children. A Palette of Rust and Dreams was inspired by her love of trains. All of the photographs were taken on the train, along the tracks and in the passenger stations of the piedmont region of North Carolina. This ongoing body of work includes an audio component with passenger and railroad worker interviews. These intimate photographs explore the space between destinations as they depict travelers and railroad workers bearing witness to a world and landscape both ephemeral and historic.

Karen's work is currently on display at Allday Cafe in Research Triangle Park, NC.

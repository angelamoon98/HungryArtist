Naoko Morisawa

About Naoko

Naoko Morisawaâ€™s artwork is hand-made of thousands of very small slices of natural and oil-dyed (wood) chips on board. She likes to incorporate the patterns in the wood and enhance them with oil-stain. The variety of wood grain is very beautiful and the pattern is never the same. The combinations of natural and oil-stained grains create interesting shadows and impressions. Her imagery comes from common items: a cupcake, shoes, a wave, and waterfalls etc. When seen from a distance, my artwork looks like a painting. The details of the work and mosaic technique slowly emerge when the viewer comes closer.

Naoko's work is currently on display at Oddfellows Cafe & Bar in Seattle, WA.
